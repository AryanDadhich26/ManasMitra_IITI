What  it  does?  
●  The  SLUMS  (Saint  Louis  University  Mental  Status)  test  is  a  brief,  11-question  
screening
 
tool
 
used
 
to
 
detect
 
early
 
signs
 
of
 
dementia
 
and
 
mild
 
cognitive
 
impairment
.
 
Taking
 
about
 
7
 
minutes
 
to
 
complete,
 
it
 
assesses
 
memory,
 
attention,
 
language,
 
and
 
executive
 
functions
 
by
 
asking
 
patients
 
to
 
perform
 
tasks
 
like
 
drawing
 
a
 
clock,
 
recalling
 
short
 
stories,
 
and
 
naming
 
animals.
 
 
How  does  it  do  it?  
●  ask  the  patient  a  set  of  11  questions,  with  the  SLUMS  score  ranging  from  0  to  
30  ○  Questions  one  to  three:  Orientation  ○  Questions  four  and  seven:  Short-term  memory  and  delayed  recall  ○  Question  five:  Numeric  calculation  ○  Question  six:  Language  and  verbal  fluency  ○  Question  eight:  Working  memory  ○  Questions  nine  and  ten:  Executive  function  and  visuospatial  ability  ○  Question  eleven:  Immediate  recall,  executive  function,  and  
extrapolation/abstract
 
thinking
 ●  Scoring:  SLUMS  scoring is  broken  down  into  two  scales,  one  for  patients  without  a  complete  high  school  education  and  another  those  with  a  highschool  
education
 
or
 
greater.
 
 ○  For  patients  who  have  not  completed  grade  12  or  equivalent  
education:
 ■  25–30:  Regular  cognition  ■  20–24:  Mild  cognitive  impairment  ■  1–19:  Signs  of  dementia  ○  For  patients  who  have  completed  grade  12  or  equivalent  
education:
 ■  27–30:  Regular  cognition  ■  21–26:  Mild  cognitive  impairment  
--- Page 22 ---
■  1–20:  Signs  of  dementia  
However,  it  should  be  noted  that  SLUMS  results  can  be  skewed  if  
the
 
patient
 
has:
 
●  Language  or  cultural  barriers  with  the  physician.  ●  Vision  or  hearing  impairments.  ●  Existing  mental  conditions  
Here’s  how  each  question  is  scored  according  to  the  Alberta  guide:  
1.  Day  of  the  week  →  1  point  (exact  answer  only)  2.  Year  →  1  point  (exact)  3.  Province  (or  State/Country)  →  1  point  (exact)  4.  Remember  5  objects  (Apple,  Pen,  Tie,  House,  Car)  →  Not  scored  here
